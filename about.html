THE BEGINNING

Barbara studied at the Academy of Fine Arts in Gdansk, North of Poland. Her professors at didn't want to hear anything of portraiture those days. They taught the conceptual art, avangard. After the diploma in 1972, she moved to Venice to continue studies at the famous Accademia di Belle Arti. At the first exhibit in Rome, Barbara presented her abstract paintings and only one portrait. The visitors have gathered only to admire the portrait. After few days, telephone started ringing...

In Italy, she have painted the famous Prince Raimondo Orsini, then considered the most handsome man in Italy. As well as, the honored writer Alberto Moravia. Then continued her promising career in London painting His Holiness Pope John Paul II, Her Majesty Queen Elisabeth The Queen Mother, and many more magnificent personas.

UNIQUE PORTRAITS

Truth and pleasure, true pleasure and pleasurable ture. These phrases describe uniqueness of Barbara’s portraits, which is ensured by her individual approach. She believes that a good portrait moves by its ambiance, inspires, makes us intrigued, makes us wonder - who is there behind the face? She presents her sitters the way they would like to see themselves. The best pose, the most elegant outfit. Emphasis on those characteristics gives the most beautiful about the person and the soul. A favorite and distinctive technique for Barbara is pastels, they allow her to create delicate or strong shadows on the faces: for women they add mystery, and for men they bring out all the strengths.

POLISH SYMBOL

Beside painting and piano playing, Barbara actively contributes to Polish society in London. At the Polish Hearth Club - the centre of social and cultural life for the Polish community - she helps to maintain and keep alive Polish history and identity. In certain period she was the Chairperson of this organisation. Due to her social activities she was awarded and listed among the 50 citizens of Polish ethnic background, who made a significant impact on the life and history of the British Isles, among General Anders, Joseph Conrad, King Canute and Roman Polanski.
